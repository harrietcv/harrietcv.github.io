Gender

Just like cases and numbers (whether the word is singular or plural), gender is another thing you have to take into account when dealing with German grammar.

There are three genders: feminine, masculine and neuter.

The feminine and plural forms can look similar, so beware!



Gender also dictates which article the word uses - for example, 'cat' is feminine in German, so its definite and indefinite articles are 'die' and eine respectively.

There's nothing else to say really except you need to learn them. Even if I listed the genders of every German noun I could think of, it wouldn't make much difference - there's no shortcut to memorising them all. Just put in a few minutes/hours each day, find a method that works for you and stick with it as best as you can!

Look through the cases section and apply your understanding of gender in German nouns to help make your sentences make sense.
